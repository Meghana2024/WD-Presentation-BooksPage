"Percy Jackson and the Olympians"

"Percy Jackson and the Olympians" is a series of fantasy-adventure novels by Rick Riordan. The first book, "The Lightning Thief," follows the protagonist, Percy Jackson, a young boy who discovers he is a demigod, the son of Poseidon, god of the sea. Percy embarks on a quest to prevent a war among the gods by retrieving Zeus's stolen lightning bolt. Along the way, he encounters mythical creatures, forms friendships, and learns about his true identity. The series delves into Greek mythology, blending it with modern-day settings and themes of friendship, loyalty, and identity.

In the subsequent books of the series, Percy continues to navigate the challenges of being a demigod while facing new adversaries and quests. He discovers more about his heritage, encounters other demigods, and becomes entangled in the conflicts of the gods. Each book explores different aspects of Greek mythology while maintaining a fast-paced plot filled with action, humor, and suspense. As Percy grows and matures throughout the series, he grapples with his destiny, friendships are tested, and alliances are forged as the fate of Olympus hangs in the balance.
